My Medical Career

Introduction
I am Currently the Head of Department of emergency medicine at Madadeni Regional Hospital in Newcastle KZN, South Africa.
I have been heavily involved in emergency medicine since 2015 and am a member of the Emergency Medicine Society of South Africa.
Prior to joining this department I was a General Practitioner on the East Rand of Johannesburg (Specifically a small town called Springs), and had worked as a GP for ten years.
Although I loved my GP practice I found myself yearning for the excitement of the emergency area, and have always thrived in a fast-paced, high-pressure work setting.
Before my foray as a GP, I was a medical officer in the Surgical Department of Pholosong Hospital (also in Springs), and had also completed my community service year there.
My internship year was done at George Hospital in the Western Cape.
All these experiences have shaped me into the Doctor I am today.

Education and Training
I completed my schooling at Springs Secondary School.
My medical training was done at the Medical University of South Africa (MEDUNSA) from 1997 - 2002.
I am also a certified ATLS, Advanced ACLS/PALS/POCUS provider, and have completed multiple other short course over the years relating to general practice and emergency Medicine

Professional experience
Having 20 years of experience has allowed me to see nearly the full gambit of conditions related to general and emergency practice.
My work now focuses mainly on administration, mentoring, teaching and guiding (through bedside, online and written material) the younger doctors in my department.
Please see my Portfolio page to see examples of my teaching and training programs, as well as my extensive medical writing
